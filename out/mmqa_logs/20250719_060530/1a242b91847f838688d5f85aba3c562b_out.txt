
=== FINAL SUMMARY ===

**Question**: What made up the three tiers of the Japanese political system around 1600?

**Factual Answer**: Around 1600, the Japanese political system was structured into three main tiers:

1. **Emperor**: The Emperor was at the top of the hierarchy, serving as the ceremonial and religious leader. Although the Emperor was considered a divine ruler, his power was largely symbolic during this period.

2. **Shogun**: The Shogun held the real political and military power. The Shogunate, or Bakufu, was the military government led by the Shogun. Around 1600, the Tokugawa Shogunate was established by Tokugawa Ieyasu, marking the beginning of the Edo period.

3. **Daimyo**: Below the Shogun were the Daimyo, who were powerful feudal lords controlling large areas of land. They had their own armies and were vassals to the Shogun, playing a crucial role in maintaining order and governance in their respective domains.
