The Great Seal of the United States is a powerful symbol representing the nation's values and identity.

1. **Symbolism**: The bald eagle, a central figure on the seal, embodies strength and freedom, key attributes associated with the United States. Its presence on the seal underscores the nation's commitment to these ideals.

2. **Elements and Arrangement**: The seal's design is symmetrical, with the eagle holding an olive branch in its right talon and a bundle of arrows in its left. This balance signifies the country's dual commitment to peace and readiness for defense.

3. **Motto and Stars**: Above the eagle, the motto "E PLURIBUS UNUM" translates to "Out of many, one," reflecting the unity of the states. The circle of stars surrounded by clouds symbolizes the emergence of a new nation.

4. **Historical Context**: The Great Seal, adopted in 1782, has been a consistent emblem of American governance and diplomacy, appearing on official documents and currency, reinforcing its significance in national identity.
