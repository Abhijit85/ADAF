**Calculation**:
1. **Identify the Operating Profit for 2011 and 2012**:
   - Operating profit in 2012: $1083 million
   - Operating profit in 2011: $1063 million

2. **Calculate the Change in Operating Profit**:
   \[
   \text{Change in Operating Profit} = \text{Operating Profit in 2012} - \text{Operating Profit in 2011} = 1083 - 1063 = 20 \text{ million}
   \]

3. **Calculate the Growth Rate**:
   \[
   \text{Growth Rate} = \left( \frac{\text{Change in Operating Profit}}{\text{Operating Profit in 2011}} \right) \times 100 = \left( \frac{20}{1063} \right) \times 100
   \]

4. **Perform the Division and Multiplication**:
   \[
   \text{Growth Rate} \approx \left( 0.01882 \right) \times 100 \approx 1.88\%
   \]
