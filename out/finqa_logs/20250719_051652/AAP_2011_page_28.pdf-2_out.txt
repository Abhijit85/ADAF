
=== FINAL SUMMARY ===

**Question**: What is the ROI of an investment in the S&P 500 Index from December 30, 2006, to January 3, 2009?

**Calculation**: 
To calculate the Return on Investment (ROI), we use the formula:

\[
\text{ROI} = \left(\frac{\text{Final Value} - \text{Initial Value}}{\text{Initial Value}}\right) \times 100
\]

Given:
- **Initial Value (December 30, 2006)**: $100.00
- **Final Value (January 3, 2009)**: $65.70

Substitute the values into the formula:

\[
\text{ROI} = \left(\frac{65.70 - 100.00}{100.00}\right) \times 100
\]

Calculate the difference between the final and initial values:

\[
65.70 - 100.00 = -34.30
\]

Divide by the initial value:

\[
\frac{-34.30}{100.00} = -0.343
\]

Convert to a percentage:

\[
-0.343 \times 100 = -34.30\%
\]

**Answer**: The ROI for an investment in the S&P 500 Index from December 30, 2006, to January 3, 2009, is -34.30%.
